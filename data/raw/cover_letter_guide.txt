Cover Letter Quick Tips

1. Address the hiring manager by name. It’s worth doing a little research to find a real person you can speak directly to. If you can’t find that name, it’s okay to use “To whom it may concern.”

2. Customize your letter for each application. Spend a few minutes looking at the job posting, the company’s LinkedIn Page, and their website. Add a few salient specifics to your letter each time.

3. Highlight transferable skills. Your resume lists your job history, but here’s a chance to show how you can use what you’ve learned from previous jobs to rock your new position.

4. Show what you can add to their culture. Your research should give you a good idea of the company’s values and office culture. You know what it might be like to work with them—show them what it’s like working with you.

5. Showcase achievements. Your resume should already focus more on achievements than responsibilities, but your cover letter is a chance to give yourself an extra shoutout. Highlight one or two accomplishments you’re particularly proud of or that are particularly relevant to this employer.

The LinkedIn Learning course, Cover Letter Tips, offers even more advice, including what to avoid and how to address potential “problem areas” in your resume.
